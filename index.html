Mine Electrical Calculation Aid

Cable, conductor, overload & short-circuit sizing for mine electrical inspection

Single-Motor Branch Circuit

Enter the motor, and this computes full-load current, the 125% conductor/overload basis, a compliant cable size, and short-circuit protection settings for thermal (fuse / thermal CB) and instantaneous-trip (magnetic) circuit breakers. Verify every value against the nameplate, the regulation, and manufacturer data.

Feeder / Multi-Motor Circuit

Feeder conductors are sized per NEC 430.24 — (largest motor FLC × 125%) + FLC of every other motor — and the feeder short-circuit device is limited per NEC 430.62(A) (surface) or NEC-1968 430-62(a) (underground coal, 30 CFR 75.900 and 30 CFR 75.518-1) to the largest branch-circuit device rating + FLC of every other motor. Surface rows fed by an adjustable-speed drive (VFD) enter the conductor calculation at the drive's rated input current instead of the motor FLC (NEC 430.122(D)); the 430.62 device computation keeps motor FLC per that section's text. Motor Overload / Heater

Overload (running) protection is sized on the motor's nameplate full-load amps (FLA) — NEC 430.6(A)(2) / 430.32, not the NEC table FLC. Type-FH heaters are published as nameplate-FLA ranges that already incorporate the 125% basis, so the heater is the one whose range contains the nameplate FLA. Do not multiply FLA by 1.25 first — that lands a size or two high. The correct range also depends on the starter size, whether the relay is ambient-compensated, whether the starter is open or enclosed, and whether the overload is a block with three heaters or a single-pole unit.

Why there is no 125% multiplication here. The manufacturer's chart is already built so that a heater chosen on the motor's actual nameplate FLA trips at or below the NEC 430.32 limit. Entering the chart with FLA × 1.25 double-counts the margin and yields an oversized heater. The 125% figure is still shown below as the regulatory ceiling for checking an already installed heater.

Magnetic Breaker Trip-Range Helper

For a magnetic (instantaneous) circuit breaker, the short-circuit pickup is set between 700% (starting point) and 1300% of motor FLC. Enter FLC to see the target window, the matching breaker frames, and the dial setting to use.

Conductor Ampacity & Cable Sizing

Enter a required ampacity (typically motor FLC × 125%) to highlight the smallest compliant cable, or just browse the tables. Underground values are mining-cable ampacities calculated for underground ambient temperature factors; surface values follow NEC Table 310.16.

Where the numbers come from. Underground cable ampacities are the ICEA tables reproduced in Appendix A of the MSHA Electrical Inspection Procedures Handbook (PH20-V-5), pages 71–78 of the PDF, transcribed as published. Type-FH overload heater ranges are the Cutler-Hammer / Westinghouse selection tables for starter sizes 00–6 (size 7 and larger has no published chart — advise the motor's full-load current to the manufacturer). Both are reproduced verbatim; where the published source contains an evident typographical error, the correction and its basis are listed below rather than applied silently.

Transcription notes & known source anomalies

  • Type-FH chart, five corrected cells. FH24 non-comp-enclosed single-pole printed 222 – 2.43, read as 2.22 – 2.43 (FH23 ends at 2.21). FH32 same column printed 4.62 – 4.9, read as 4.62 – 4.99 (FH33 starts at 5.0). FH54 block-3 printed 31.5 – 34, read as 31.5 – 34.5 (FH55 starts at 34.6). FH18 non-comp-enclosed block-3 printed 1.04 – 1.16, read as 1.04 – 1.13 (FH19 starts at 1.14, and the other three columns are contiguous). FH07 non-comp-enclosed block-3 printed 0.38 – 0.39, read as 0.36 – 0.39 (FH06 ends at 0.35, FH08 starts at 0.40, and neighbouring ranges in that column all span 0.03–0.04 A — as printed it would leave 0.36–0.37 A covered by no heater). Every other range in the chart is contiguous with its neighbour at the chart's printed decimal resolution, with no overlaps, which is what makes these five detectable. (An entered FLA can still fall numerically between one range's printed top and the next range's bottom — for example 41.55 A between 41.5 and 41.6. That is a rounding artifact of the printed endpoints, which the calculators report as "between ranges"; it is different from the whole printed values these five misprints left uncovered.)
  • ICEA tables, two corrected cells (with their other-ambient companions). Table A-1, 2 AWG, 3/C shielded 15001–25000 V printed 178, read as 168: Table A-4 rates the identical construction and ambient at 168 A, and in every other size this column sits 2–5 A above the 8001–15000 V column, where 178 would jump 14 A. The 30 °C and 20 °C companions follow at 185 and 198. Table A-4, 300 kcmil-row aluminum 8001–15000 V printed 393, read as 403: as printed it breaks the rise across voltage classes that every other row shows (395 → 403 → 404); the A-5 / A-6 companions follow at 443 and 476. Each correction is the value demanded by the handbook's own cross-tables and row patterns; the first (178 → 168) lowers a printed ampacity and is therefore also the conservative reading.

Security & privacy

  • Everything runs locally. There is no server component and no code executes anywhere but on your device.
  • No third-party network use. The page loads no external scripts, styles, fonts, images, or trackers. A Content-Security-Policy blocks every outbound connection except to this site's own origin, which is used only to keep the offline copy fresh, so nothing you enter can leak. The only external links on the page are to the GNU license text and to this project's source code on GitHub; nothing is loaded from either unless you click it.
  • Works offline. A service worker keeps the latest copy of this page on your device. With a connection, every load fetches the live version; without one, the saved copy opens instead. The copy is only as fresh as your last visit with a connection. On iPhone/iPad, use Share → Add to Home Screen and open it from there: Safari otherwise deletes the offline copy after 7 days without a visit.
  • Minimal storage. The page stores two things: a session-scoped "disclaimer acknowledged" flag (browser sessionStorage), so the startup notice appears once per session instead of on every load, and the offline copy of the page itself (Cache Storage). Neither holds anything you enter.
